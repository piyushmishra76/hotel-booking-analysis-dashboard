# 📊 Hotel Booking Analysis Report

---

## 1. Introduction
Objective
This project focuses on analyzing hotel booking data to understand customer behavior, pricing patterns, and cancellation trends. The goal is to extract meaningful insights and build an interactive dashboard using Microsoft Excel.

---

## 2. Dataset Overview

The dataset contains information about hotel bookings, including:

- Booking details (lead time, arrival date)
- Customer information (country, customer type)
- Pricing data (ADR - Average Daily Rate)
- Booking outcomes (cancellation status)
- Room allocation details

Each row represents a single booking.

---

## 3. Data Cleaning

Before analysis, the dataset was cleaned to ensure accuracy:

- Handled NULL values in columns like `agent` and `company`
- Removed unnecessary or irrelevant columns
- Ensured correct data types for numerical and categorical variables
- Verified consistency in categorical fields (e.g., market segment, deposit type)

---

## 4. Analysis & Insights

### 🔹 Booking Trends
Booking demand varies across months, indicating seasonal patterns in hotel reservations.

---

### 🔹 Cancellation Analysis
- Higher lead time is associated with a higher probability of cancellation
- Deposit type significantly impacts cancellation behavior:
  - No deposit → higher cancellations
  - Refundable → lower cancellations

---

### 🔹 Customer Behavior
- Repeat guests have lower cancellation rates, indicating higher reliability
- However, repeat guests tend to have lower ADR, possibly due to loyalty discounts

---

### 🔹 Lead Time Impact
Customers who book far in advance are more likely to cancel compared to last-minute bookings.

---

### 🔹 Pricing (ADR) Distribution
- Most bookings fall within a mid-range price
- The distribution is right-skewed, with a few high-value bookings acting as outliers

---

### 🔹 Room Allocation
- Upgraded bookings often have lower ADR, suggesting complimentary upgrades
- Downgraded bookings sometimes retain higher ADR, indicating pricing is fixed at booking time

---

### 🔹 Customer Composition
- Majority of bookings are made by adults only
- Family bookings (with children/babies) are less frequent

---

### 🔹 Market Segment Analysis
Different booking channels (Direct, Corporate, Online TA) show variations in pricing and booking behavior.

---

### 🔹 Customer History Impact
- Customers with previous cancellations are more likely to cancel again
- Customers with successful booking history are more reliable

---

## 5. Dashboard Overview

An interactive dashboard was created using Excel with the following features:

- KPI cards (Total Bookings, Cancellation Rate, ADR, Lead Time)
- Multiple charts for trends and comparisons
- Slicers for filtering data by hotel, market segment, and time

---

## 6. Conclusion

The analysis highlights that customer behavior, booking timing, and pricing strategies significantly influence hotel performance. Understanding these factors can help improve decision-making and reduce cancellation risks.
